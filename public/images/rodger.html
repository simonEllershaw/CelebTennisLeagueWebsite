    Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal and Bryan brothers claim from Serena Williams

    ROGER FEDERER, Rafael Nadal and the Bryan brothers are a huge draw for fans, says Serena Williams.

    By Alex Wood
    PUBLISHED: 08:05, Tue, Dec 11, 2018 | UPDATED: 08:17, Tue, Dec 11, 2018
    0

    Mike and Bob Bryan will be competing at next month's Sydney International.

    The duo are making their comeback after Bob was sidelined by injury in Madrid earlier this year.

    Tennis legend Williams reckons the American doubles stars attract spectators - just like heavyweights Federer and Nadal

    She said: “People come out to the tennis to see Roger Federer, Nadal, the Bryan brothers.

    Related articles

    • Roger Federer: Tennis superstar told he DESERVES special treatment

    Serena Williams says the Bryan brothers are popular with tennis fans

    “They are just as big a card as anyone else.

    “That's really never been done, and it's really awesome.

    “What makes them special on the court? Their energy is just unbelievable.

    “Their energy is so great. Whether they are up or they're down, you can't tell.

    Roger Federer has been discussed by Serena Williams (Image: GETTY)

    “It seems like they're just having the time of their lives on the court.

    “For me personally, I love the dynamic that they're brothers.

    “I can really relate to that because I play doubles with my sister.

    “We don't have that energy, but we definitely have so much fun out there, and I feel just like on that aspect we have so much to relate to.”

    Swiss superstar Federer was slammed by Julien Benneteau last month for how much influence he wields in the game.

    Related articles

    • Roger Federer backed over problem involving Novak Djokovic

    Serena Williams says the Bryan brothers are popular with tennis fans (Image: GETTY)

    Benneteau claimed Federer put his involvement in the Laver Cup over the Davis Cup.

    He then accused the International Tennis Federation of being “weak” in their handling of the two events.

    The 36-year-old also highlighted a conflict of interest in Australian Open tournament director Craig Tiley being involved in the Laver Cup and deciding Federer’s match schedule.

    Tommy Haas, however, has suggested that some compromise is needed given Federer’s huge popularity.

    “It’s tough in our sport, obviously," he explained.

    “You have to give respect to the players that have achieved a certain amount of success over the years.

    Roger Federer was slammed by Julien Benneteau (Image: GETTY)

    “You have to take that in respect of what people want to see as well.

    “Obviously being a tennis player and being on the tour myself for 20 years, I know what it feels like when you want to play at a certain time and it doesn’t quite work out that way or you wish you could play on another court and it doesn’t quite work out this way as well.

    “It is what it is, it’s tough.

    “Facts are facts and certain things have to be the way they are.

    “Trying, also, to be in charge of the schedule we only have to take all of those things into effect and try to please everybody as much as we can, which is quite tough to do but I can’t speak for the Australian Open – obviously if he did play 12 or 13 of his last 14 matches at 7.30pm, that’s prime time.

    “Everybody loves Roger around the world.

    “You have to take those sorts of things into account and in the end that’s the tournament director’s choice.”
